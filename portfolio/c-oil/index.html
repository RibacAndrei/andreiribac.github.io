The science of CBD

Cannabis contains over 500 compounds, the most notorious being THC (tetrahydrocannabinol) and CBD (cannabidiol), both cannabinoids. Unlike THC, CBD does not produce a high. It has excellent medical potential, which explains its growing popularity in recent years.

CBD is a cannabinoid with a unique appeal, as it may have the potential to regulate the sleep-wake cycle. CBD is not by itself a direct sedative, as some other sleep aids are. On the contrary, CBD may actually promote wakefulness during daytime hours. This complex interaction of CBD is partly due to the endogenous cannabinoid system.

Some of CBD properties have been extensively studied, while others are only just beginning to interest scientists. Either way, the abundance of therapeutic applications justifies further research into the effects of CBD and how it can be applied in medicine.
